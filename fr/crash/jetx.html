How JetX works

Each JetX round begins with a jet aircraft appearing on screen. A multiplicateur counter starts at 1.00x and climbs as the jet ascends. You must clique sur le cash-out bouton before the jet disappears, or "crashes" and the round ends, to collect your stake multiplied by the displayed value. If you fail to cash-out before the crash, your entire bet is lost.

JetX supports two simultaneous bet machines à sous per player. You can allocate a larger stake to one machine à sous and a smaller hedge to the second. Each machine à sous has its own independent cash-out bouton, so you can secure the second bet at 2x while the first remains active and climbing toward a higher target.

Bet placement closes a few seconds before each round begins. The crash point for every round is determined by a équitable vérifiable algorithm seeded before betting opens, so the outcome cannot be altered once misers are placed. The server seed hash is displayed before the round, allowing post-round verification.

SmartSoft Gaming publishes historical round data in-game. You can scan previous crash points to see the distribution, though past results have no predictive value for future rounds. The multiplicateur at which any given round crashes is independent of all previous results.

Stratégie et bankroll pour JetX

The two fundamental approaches in JetX are low-multiplicateur disciplined play and high-multiplicateur hunting. Understanding the mathematics of each is important before committing argent réel.

Low-multiplicateur strategy (2x auto-cash-out): Setting an auto-cash-out at 2.00x means you need the jet to survive past 2x pour gagner. At a 97% RTP, a theoretical 2x game would require the bet to succeed roughly 48.5% of the time across a large sample. In reality, rounds crash below 2x frequently enough that disciplined flat betting at 2x produces slow bankroll erosion over volume. The advantage is that losing streaks are shorter and each win is defined. Use a unit stake of no more than 1-2% of your session bankroll.

Side bet hedge: The second bet machine à sous allows a practical hybrid. Place a smaller auto-cash-out at 2x on machine à sous two, and manually decide when to exit on machine à sous one. This reduces the impact of a crash just below your main target, though it also reduces overall return per unit staked.

High-multiplicateur targeting: Aiming for 10x, 50x, or higher requires accepting that most rounds will not reach those levels. The correct approach is to size stakes proportionally smaller, keeping potential losses per round to 0.5-1% of bankroll. Never increase taille de la mise after a losing streak expecting a "due" high multiplicateur. Each round is independent.

Session discipline: Set a loss limit before each session (par exemple, 20 units) and a win-goal (10 units profit). When either is reached, end the session. JetX moves quickly and the absence of a visible timer makes it easy to extend sessions beyond rational limits.

Équitable vérifiable verification in JetX

SmartSoft Gaming implements a dual-seed équitable vérifiable system. Before each round, the server generates a seed and publishes its SHA-256 hash publicly. You can also set a client seed. The crash point for the round is derived from combining both seeds through a deterministic algorithm, so neither the player nor the operator can alter the result after the hash is shown.

After the round ends, the server reveals the original seed. You can independently hash it and confirm it matches the pre-round hash, then run the same derivation algorithm to verify the crash point. SmartSoft provides a verification tool within the game interface. Any discrepancy between the published hash and the revealed seed would be detectable instantanély.
