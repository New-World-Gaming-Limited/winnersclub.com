Comment fonctionne Aviatrix

Aviatrix follows the fondé en crash-game format: a plane appears, a multiplicateur counter begins at 1.00x and rises, and you must cash-out before the plane flies away. The game ends instantanély when the plane disappears, and any uncashed bets are lost. The distinguishing feature of Aviatrix is the social layer built on top of this core mechanic.

A live classement displays the top cash-outs achieved by all active players in the current session. A shared bet feed shows every bet placed and every cash-out executed in real time, which gives a sense of community activity without revealing strategic informations about upcoming crash points.

NFT plane skins allow you to customise the aircraft with unique visual identifiers. These are purely cosmetic: the RNG determines the crash point independently of which plane skin is active. Skins can be acquired, traded, or held as blockchain assets outside the game, but their presence or absence has no effect on your valeur attendue.

Each round uses a équitable vérifiable system. The server seed hash is publicly shown before betting opens. After the round, the seed is revealed and can be combined with your client seed in a publicly documented algorithm to reproduce the crash point. This confirms that the crash point was fixed before any bets were placed.

Stratégie et bankroll pour Aviatrix

Aviatrix's 97% RTP matches JetX and the Spribe titles, making it one of the more player-favourable crash options at Stake. The social features are engaging but can create misleading impressions: when you see another player cash-out at 10x on the classement, that result has no predictive value for the next round.

Avoiding social-proof bias: The public bet feed shows large wins prominently. This is a display feature, not a signal about upcoming rounds. Resist the temptation to increase your stake or cash-out target based on what other players are doing. Each round is independent.

Low-multiplicateur auto-cash-out: A 2x auto-cash-out in Aviatrix targets a break-even success rate of approximately 48.5% at 97% RTP. This is a disciplined, low-variance approach for players who want defined, repeatable outcomes. Use a flat stake of 1-2% of session bankroll per round. Accept that losing streaks of 4-6 rounds will occur during normal play.

Targeting medium multiplicateurs: Setting auto-cash-out between 3x and 8x reduces win frequency but increases the payout per successful round. With a 97% RTP, the expected return per bet is constant regardless of target multiplicateur over a large sample, but variance increases with target size. Reduce taille de la mise proportionally.

Big-play sessions: Going for 25x, 50x, or 100x requires staking very small amounts per round. A realistic approach is 0.25-0.5% of bankroll per round at these targets. The probability of hitting 100x in a single session with a reasonable number of rounds is low; treat it as entertainment expenditure rather than an income strategy.

Équitable vérifiable verification in Aviatrix

Aviatrix utilise un système cryptographique à double graine. Le serveur génère une graine aléatoire avant chaque round et publie son hash. Vous fournissez une graine client. Le point de crash est dérivé de la combinaison des deux graines via un algorithme documenté, ce qui signifie qu'aucune des parties ne peut modifier le résultat après la publication du hash.

After each round, the server seed is revealed. You can hash it independently to confirm it matches the pre-round disclosure, then apply the derivation algorithm to confirm the crash point. Aviatrix provides a verification interface within the game and also documents the algorithm publicly for independent checking.
