كيف يعمل Plinko

Plinko drops a ball from the top of a pyramid of pegs. At each row, the ball bounces left or right with equal probability (approximately 50/50 per peg). The number of rows you select determines how many pegs the ball passes through. With 8 rows, there are 9 possible landing slots. With 16 rows, there are 17. The central slots are always the most probable landing zone due to the binomial distribution of left/right deflections. Edge slots are geometrically unlikely and carry the highest مضاعفs.

The risk setting changes the values assigned to each slot, not the physical probability of landing there. On low risk, the edge مضاعفs are modest and the centre مضاعفs are slightly above 1x, making most drops return close to the staked amount. On high risk, the centre slots pay less than 1x and the edge slots can reach 1,000x, but the odds of reaching an edge slot remain the same binomial probability regardless of the risk setting.
