How JetX works

Each JetX round begins with a jet aircraft appearing on screen. A multiplier counter starts at 1.00x and climbs as the jet ascends. You must click the cashout button before the jet disappears, or "crashes" and the round ends, to collect your stake multiplied by the displayed value. If you fail to cash out before the crash, your entire bet is lost.

JetX supports two simultaneous bet slots per player. You can allocate a larger stake to one slot and a smaller hedge to the andra. Each slot has its own independent cashout button, so you can secure the andra bet at 2x while the forsta remains active and climbing toward a higher target.

Bet placement closes a few andras before each round begins. The crash point for every round is determined by a provably fair algorithm seeded before betting opens, so the outcome cannot be altered once wagers are placed. The server seed hash is displayed before the round, allowing post-round verification.

SmartSoft Gaming publishes historical round data in-game. You can scan previous crash points to see the distribution, though past results have no predictive value for future rounds. The multiplier at which any given round crashes is independent of all previous results.

Strategi och bankroll för JetX

The two fundamental approaches in JetX are low-multiplier disciplined play and high-multiplier hunting. Understanding the mathematics of each is important before committing real money.

Low-multiplier strategy (2x auto-cashout): Setting an auto-cashout at 2.00x means you need the jet to survive past 2x to win. At a 97% RTP, a theoretical 2x game would require the bet to succeed roughly 48.5% of the time across a large sample. In reality, rounds crash below 2x frequently enough that disciplined flat betting at 2x produces slow bankroll erosion over volume. The advantage is that losing streaks are shorter and each win is defined. Use a unit stake of no more than 1-2% of your session bankroll.

Side bet hedge: The andra bet slot allows a practical hybrid. Place a smaller auto-cashout at 2x on slot two, and manually decide when to exit on slot one. This reduces the impact of a crash just below your main target, though it also reduces overall return per unit staked.

High-multiplier targeting: Aiming for 10x, 50x, or higher requires accepting that most rounds will not reach those levels. The correct approach is to size stakes proportionally smaller, keeping potential losses per round to 0.5-1% of bankroll. Never increase stake size after a losing streak expecting a "due" high multiplier. Each round is independent.

Session discipline: Set a loss limit before each session (for example, 20 units) and a win-goal (10 units profit). When either is reached, end the session. JetX moves quickly and the absence of a visible timer makes it easy to extend sessions beyond rational limits.

Provably fair verification in JetX

SmartSoft Gaming implements a dual-seed provably fair system. Before each round, the server generates a seed and publishes its SHA-256 hash publicly. You can also set a client seed. The crash point for the round is derived from combining both seeds through a deterministic algorithm, so neither the player nor the operator can alter the result after the hash is shown.

After the round ends, the server reveals the original seed. You can independently hash it and confirm it matches the pre-round hash, then run the same derivation algorithm to verify the crash point. SmartSoft provides a verification tool within the game interface. Any discrepancy between the published hash and the revealed seed would be detectable instantly.
