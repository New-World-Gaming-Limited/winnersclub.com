Hur Plinko fungerar

Plinko drops a ball from the top of a pyramid of pegs. At each row, the ball bounces left or right with equal probability (approximately 50/50 per peg). After passing through all rows, the ball lands in one of the multiplier slots at the bottom of the board. The multiplier in that slot is applied to your stake.

The number of rows you select determines how many pegs the ball passes through. With 8 rows, there are 9 possible landing slots. With 16 rows, there are 17. The central slots are always the most probable landing zone due to the binomial distribution of left/right deflections. Edge slots are geometrically unlikely and carry the highest multipliers.

The risk setting changes the values assigned to each slot, not the physical probability of landing there. On low risk, the edge multipliers are modest and the centre multipliers are slightly above 1x, making most drops return close to the staked amount. On high risk, the centre slots pay less than 1x and the edge slots can reach 1,000x, but the odds of reaching an edge slot remain the same binomial probability regardless of the risk setting.

Each ball drop is independently seeded via Spribe's provably fair system. Auto-bet allows you to queue multiple drops at a chosen stake, with optional stop-on-win or stop-on-loss rules.

Strategi och bankroll för Plinko

Low risk setting: Low risk at 16 rows produces the tightest payout distribution. Most drops land in slots paying between 0.5x and 1.5x. The 97% RTP means the house takes 3% on average, but variance is low enough that bankroll swings are modest. This is suitable for spelare who want to extend session time and minimise large loss spikes. Set auto-bet with a small flat stake and a session loss limit.

Medium risk tradeoffs: Medium risk widens the multiplier range, increasing the frequency of 0x or near-zero outcomes while adding occasional 5x-20x hits. Expected value per bet is unchanged (still negative by the house edge), but session outcomes become less predictable. Use a smaller stake than you would at low risk.

High risk and the max-multiplier fallacy: High risk at 16 rows offers the largest advertised multipliers, but the probability of landing in a high-multiplier edge slot is very low. A ball passing 16 rows has a roughly 0.003% chance of landing in the outermost slot. Over a session of 100 drops, the expected number of times you hit that slot is 0.003. The high-risk setting does not increase expected return; it concentrates potential profit into very rare events. Use only if the entertainment value of occasional large hits outweighs the more frequent below-stake returns.

Row count: Mer rows increase variance. 8 rows at high risk is a more moderate version of high-risk play; 16 rows at high risk is the maximum variance configuration. 12 rows at medium risk is a mid-point many spelare use for balanced sessions.

Session management: Set a fixed number of drops per session rather than a time limit. Plinko moves quickly on auto-bet and it is easy to exhaust a bankroll faster than expected. A 100-drop session at 1% of bankroll per drop gives a structured, measurable session.

Provably fair verification in Plinko

Spribe's Plinko uses the same dual-seed provably fair system as their other titles. Before each ball drop, the server seed hash is published. After the drop, the server reveals the seed, allowing you to hash it and confirm it matches. The ball path through each row is then deterministically reproducible from the seeds, confirming the outcome was fixed before your bet was accepted.

Spribe provides an in-game verification panel. You can also input the seeds into an independently published algorithm to reproduce every left/right deflection and confirm the landing slot. This makes Plinko fully verifiable without relying on trust in the operator.

FAQ

What is the RTP of Plinko at Stake?

Plinko by Spribe has an RTP of up to 97%, which applies at the low-risk setting. Higher risk levels maintain the same theoretical RTP but redistribute payouts toward rarer, larger wins.

What does the risk level setting do in Plinko?

The risk level (low, medium, high) controls how payouts are distributed across the multiplier slots at the bottom. Low risk gives frequent small wins with a low maximum multiplier. High risk gives a higher maximum multiplier but far fewer winning slots; most drops land in low or zero payout positions.

How many rows can I choose in Plinko?

Plinko allows 8 to 16 rows. Mer rows mean more peg interactions and a more spread-out multiplier distribution. The central slots at the bottom remain the most likely landing zone regardless of row count.

Is Plinko provably fair?

Yes. Spribe Plinko uses a provably fair seed system. The server seed hash is published before each drop, and after the round you can verify the outcome using the revealed seed.

Can I automate bets in Plinko?

Yes. Plinko supports an auto-bet mode where you specify the number of balls to drop automatiskt at a fixed stake, which is useful for testing a setting over multiple iterations.
