How to deposit using NEFT at Stake

  1. Log in and open the Cashier. Select "Deposit," then choose NEFT from the payment method list.
  2. Copy the Stake beneficiary details. The cashier displays the account number, IFSC code, and account name. Screenshot this screen.
  3. Log in to your bank's net banking or mobile app. Add Stake as a NEFT beneficiary using the details from step 2. Beneficiary addition may require a 30-minute bank cool-down on first-time additions.
  4. Initiate the NEFT transfer. Select NEFT, choose the Stake beneficiary, enter the amount (minimum 500 INR), and include your Stake user ID in the payment remarks field. Confirm with your net banking password or OTP.
  5. Wait for the batch to settle. NEFT batches run every 30 minutes. You will get a UTR confirmation SMS from your bank. Your Stake wallet updates after the batch settles, typically within 30 to 120 minutes. Keep the UTR in case you need to follow up with support.

How to withdraw via NEFT from Stake

  1. Verify your identity on Stake. Upload PAN card or Aadhaar and a bank proof document before your first withdrawal.
  2. Open Cashier and click Withdraw. Select NEFT.
  3. Enter your bank account number, IFSC code, and account holder name. These must match your bank records exactly.
  4. Enter the withdrawal amount. NEFT withdrawals have no system-level cap, but Stake's risk team may request additional verification for very large withdrawals.
  5. Submit. Stake queues your withdrawal request. NEFT credits arrive in 2 to 12 hours depending on when the next batch settles at your bank. Your bank sends an SMS on credit.

Limitlar va muddatlar

RBI removed the NEFT per-transaction ceiling in December 2019, meaning there is no cap on individual NEFT transfers at the network level. However, your bank may still impose daily transfer limits through net banking (common caps are 5 lakh to 25 lakh per day depending on the bank and account type). Check your bank's net banking settings under "Transaction Limits" to see your specific cap.

NEFT operates 24/7 on all days of the year including Sundays and public holidays since December 2019. Batches settle every 30 minutes, starting at midnight. If you initiate a transfer at 2:15 PM, the batch settles at 2:30 PM and Stake should receive the funds by 3:00 PM at the latest.

Mamlakatning mavjudligi va valyuta

NEFT is available at all banks that are RBI members in India, covering virtually all public and private sector banks, regional rural banks, and cooperative banks. NEFT transfers are in INR only. Stake's receiving bank account is an Indian bank account denominated in INR.

Non-resident Indians (NRI) with NRE or NRO accounts can also use NEFT if their bank allows outward domestic transfers from these accounts.

FAQ: NEFT at Stake

How long does a NEFT deposit take at Stake?

NEFT processes in half-hourly batches 24/7. Deposits typically credit to your Stake wallet within 30 to 120 minutes after the bank initiates the transfer.

Is there a maximum limit on NEFT deposits at Stake?

RBI NEFT tranzaksiya limitini bekor qildi. Nazariy jihatdan istalgan miqdorni o'tkazishingiz mumkin. Amalda bankingiz kundalik yoki tranzaksiyaga cheklovlar qo'yishi mumkin; internet-banking sozlamalaringizni tekshiring.

What is the minimum NEFT deposit at Stake?

Minimum NEFT deposit is 500 INR.

Does Stake charge a fee for NEFT deposits?

Stake charges no fee. Your bank may charge up to 25 INR for outward NEFT transfers depending on your account type.

My NEFT deposit has not arrived after 2 hours. What should I do?

Collect your bank's NEFT UTR number and the transfer acknowledgement. Contact Stake live-chat and provide both the UTR and your Stake user ID. The payments team can locate and manually post the credit.
