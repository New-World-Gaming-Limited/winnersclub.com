How Mines works

Mines presents a 5x5 grid of 25 tiles. Before each round, you choose how many mines to hide in the grid (1 to 24), then place your tét. The mine positions are fixed by the bizonyíthatóan fair seed before you click a single tile. You do not kmost where the mines are.

Each time you click a tile and it reveals a gem (safe), your current szorzó increases and you have the option to cashout immediately, locking in that szorzó applied to your tét. If you click a mine, the round ends and your tét is lost. You can continue clicking safe tiles, accumulating a higher szorzó, until you choose to cashout or hit a mine.

The szorzó per safe tile increases with mine count. With 1 mine in a 25-tile grid, each safe tile reveals a modest szorzó; with 15 mines, each safe tile revealed carries a much larger szorzó because the risk of hitting a mine on each subsequent click is substantially higher. The játék calculates the live szorzó based on the remaining safe tiles and mines in the unrevealed area.

Auto-bet allows automatic play with a fixed tile pattern. You define which tiles to click each round, and the játék executes the sequence repeatedly. This is not a guaranteed-win strategy; it is a convenience feature that does not change the underlying valószínűség.

Stratégia és bankroll-kezelés Mines-hoz

Expected value is negative: Mines is a gambling játék, not a nyereség system. The 97% RTP means the house retains 3% of every dollar wagered over a large sample. The cashout decision: The core decision in Mines is when to cashout. From a valószínűség standpoint, the expected value of cashing out after each additional tile stays constant relative to the current szorzó because the játék's szorzó always reflects the fair szorzók of the remaining unrevealed grid. However, each additional click introduces an independent valószínűség of losing everything. This is why early cashout is mathematically defensible: you lock in a real nyereség rather than giving it back on the next click.

Mine count and EV by count: With 3 mines in a 25-tile grid, the valószínűség of surviving your first click is 22/25 (88%). Surviving two clicks: (22/25) x (21/24) = approximately 77%. Surviving three clicks: approximately 66%. By the fourth click, your survival valószínűség drops to around 56%. At each stage, the szorzó you receive if you cashout compensates for the cumulative risk taken, but the valószínűség of total loss compounds with every click. At 15 mines, the first click survival valószínűség is 10/25 (40%), and hitting the 1.5x szorzó on that click is the entire bankroll-preserving strategy.

Practical approach: Set a fixed mine count (3 to 5 mines suits most játékosok), reveal 2 to 3 tiles per round, and cashout. Repeat with a flat tét of 1-2% of session bankroll. Avoid increasing téts after wins or chasing losses with higher mine counts. The high-mine-count configurations are inherently high variance; use them only with téts sized for total loss.

How many mines can I set in Mines?

You can configure between 1 and 24 mines on a 5x5 grid. At 24 mines there is only 1 safe tile; at 1 mine you have 24 safe tiles. A common starting point for új játékosok is 3-5 mines.

Is Mines provably fair?

Yes. Spribe Mines uses a dual-seed bizonyíthatóan fair system. The mine positions are determined before you begin each játék and encoded in the server seed hash, which is published before your first click.

What happens if I hit a mine in Spribe Mines?

Hitting a mine ends the round immediately and you lose your entire tét for that round. Any szorzó accumulated from previously revealed safe tiles is forfeited.

What is the expected value of Mines at different mine counts?

The EV of any mines configuration is negative for the player (that is the nature of a house-edge játék). At higher mine counts, the potential szorzó per tile revealed increases, but so does the valószínűség of hitting a mine on each click. The optimal cashout point from a pure-valószínűség standpoint is earlier than most játékosok intuitively expect.
