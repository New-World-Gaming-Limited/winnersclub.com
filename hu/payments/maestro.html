Limitek és határidők

Maestro is a debit card, which means transactions debit directly from your bank fiókot. Napi debit limits are set by your bank, typically in the range of EUR 1,000 to EUR 5,000 per day for online transactions. Some banks have lower online Maestro limits than general debit limits; check with your bank.

Maestro card issuance was discontinued for new cards in many European piacok from 2023 as Mastercard transitioned to Debit Mastercard. However, meglévő Maestro cards remain érvényes until their expiry date. If your Maestro card is nearing expiry, contact your bank for a replacement Debit Mastercard.

For Indian játékosok holding Maestro-branded cards: Indian bank MCC blocks apply to Maestro cards the same way as Visa and Mastercard. If declined for gaming reasons, use UPI, IMPS, or crypto instead.

Ország availability and pénznem

Maestro cards are primarily issued in Europe (Germany, Italy, Netherlands, Belgium, Austria, and eastern European countries) and a few other piacok. UK banks have largely replaced Maestro with Debit Mastercard. Indian banks issue Maestro-branded RuPay cards but these operate on RuPay's domestic network. Stake accepts Maestro where supported by its payment gateway; if not listed in the cashier, use Mastercard or Visa.
