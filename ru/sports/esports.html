Охваченные турниры и соревнования

Stake follows the esports calendar from regional qualifiers through to the biggest global championship events, covering each title's most prestigious tournaments.

  • CS2 Majors - Valve's flagship CS2 Majors are the pinnacle of Counter-Strike. The Copenhagen Major 2024 drew record viewership, with Natus Vincere (NaVi) and FaZe Clan producing the most-bet matches on Stake. The 2025-26 cycle continues with Majors in multiple regions. Stake lists full tournament outright, stage-winner and match-level markets from the qualifier stage.
  • ESL Pro League (CS2) - ESL Pro League Season runs twice yearly and is the leading third-party CS2 league. Sixteen teams across two conferences compete for prize pools exceeding $1,000,000. Team Vitality, Heroic and G2 Esports are the most bet teams on Stake within the league's group stage and playoffs.
  • IEM (Intel Extreme Masters) - IEM Katowice, IEM Cologne and IEM Rio are marquee CS2 events with some of the highest live-betting volumes in esports. Stake carries full in-play map-by-map markets throughout these events.
  • The International (Dota 2) - Valve's annual Dota 2 World Championship is one of the largest prize pools in esports history. Team Spirit's back-to-back titles in 2021 and 2023 established Eastern European dominance, while Chinese and Southeast Asian teams push back each cycle. Stake covers group stage, main event and grand final with deep market selection.
  • Dota Pro Circuit (DPC) - Regional Dota 2 leagues across Europe, North America, Southeast Asia, China, South America and CIS feed into Major events. Stake lists DPC regional results and Majors as part of its year-round Dota 2 coverage.
  • League of Legends Worlds (LoL) - Riot Games' annual World Championship is the largest esport event by peak viewership. T1 led by Faker have won four Worlds titles; South Korea and China dominate the bracket each year. Stake prices all group stage games, knockouts and the grand final at Worlds and at the Mid-Season Invitational (MSI).
  • League of Legends regional leagues - LCK (Korea), LPL (China), LEC (Europe) and LCS (North America) each run split seasons that feed into Worlds. Stake covers all four major regions weekly throughout the year.
  • VCT (Valorant Champions Tour) - Riot's global Valorant ecosystem splits teams across three international leagues (Americas, EMEA, Pacific) with an annual Champions grand final. Sentinels, NRG and Team Liquid Americas have been among the most-bet clubs on Stake. The Champions event in Seoul 2024 attracted strong global wagering interest.
  • Rainbow Six Siege (R6) - Ubisoft's competitive FPS features the Six Invitational as its World Championship. Teams from Europe, North America, Brazil and Asia-Pacific compete with Stake covering all stages of the SI and the regional Six Majors.
  • Mobile Legends: Bang Bang (MLBB) - The M-Series World Championship is the largest MLBB event of the year, drawing massive viewership from Southeast Asia and beyond. Philippine teams like Echo and AP Bren attract the strongest mobile esports betting volumes on Stake.

Most popular esports betting markets

  • Match winner - Back one team to win the series (best-of-1, best-of-3, or best-of-5). The foundation market for all esports titles on Stake.
  • Map winner (CS2/Valorant) - Bet on the winner of a specific map within a series. Because teams have known map preferences, map-winner markets offer strong value for those who study map pools.
  • Map handicap - In a best-of-3 series, backing the underdog at +1.5 maps means they need to win only one map. A common accumulator building block in CS2 and Valorant.
  • Total rounds (over/under) - In CS2, a map runs to 24 rounds maximum in regulation. Betting over or under a stated line (e.g. 25.5 rounds including overtime) rewards knowledge of individual team defensive and offensive styles.
  • First map winner - Which team wins Map 1? Often priced differently from the overall series winner if one side has a clear advantage on their chosen map but is weaker across the full veto.
  • Correct score (series) - Predict the exact series scoreline: 2-0 or 2-1 in a best-of-3, or 3-0, 3-1, 3-2 in a best-of-5. Higher odds reward precise forecasting.
  • Tournament outright - Back NaVi, T1, Team Spirit or any team to win the Major, The International or Worlds before the tournament begins for maximum odds.

In-play and live esports betting

Esports delivers some of the most dynamic in-play betting in the industry. In CS2, a single clutch round can swing a map from 12-3 to a close 15-12 scoreline, and Stake's live odds update after every round, reflecting the shifting momentum in real time. A team that wins the pistol round in the second half of a map frequently converts that advantage to a round lead, creating a measurable betting signal for in-play bettors who track these patterns.

Pre-match analysis should focus on: head-to-head records in the last 30 days, map win rates broken down by side (CT vs T in CS2, attacking vs defending in Valorant), roster stability and patch version because balance changes in Dota 2 and LoL can radically shift which heroes and champions are viable. A newly buffed support hero at The International can make previously reliable picks redundant overnight.

In-play live streams for many esports events are accessible via linked Twitch and YouTube channels. Stake provides direct links from the bet slip, so you can follow the action without leaving the platform.
