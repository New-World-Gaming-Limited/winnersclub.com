The $41M hack

September 2023. North Korean state actors. Stake's response.

On September 4, 2023, approximately $41 million in cryptocurrency was drained from Stake's hot wallets. The breakdown: roughly $16M from Ethereum accounts (ETH, USDT, USDC, DAI) and $25.6M from Binance Smart Chain and Polygon (MATIC, BNB).

The attack did not involve direct private key compromise. Instead, it targeted a third-party transaction authorization service Stake used for Ethereum, Polygon, and BNB Chain operations - allowing unauthorized outbound transfers without actually holding the keys. Ed Craven stated the breach was spotted within 20 minutes and fully resolved in under 4 hours. Deposits and withdrawals were suspended temporarily and resumed the same day.

On September 7, 2023, the FBI formally attributed the hack to North Korea's Lazarus Group - the same DPRK state-sponsored unit behind the $600M Ronin Bridge attack, the $35M Atomic Wallet attack, and dozens of other 2023 crypto thefts. The stolen funds moved through a characteristic laundering chain: ETH and BSC assets swapped into unfreezable native assets, then through Squid Router, Avalanche Bridge, wrapped BTC, native Bitcoin, and eventually through the Sinbad mixer.

Craven's public statement: "The loss of funds is by no means a trivial amount, but this attack has not materially affected Stake's operations." User funds were not affected. Post-hack, Arkham-tracked reserves remained in the $300M+ range. The practical demonstration that withdrawals never stopped served as Stake's de-facto proof of solvency.
