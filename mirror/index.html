India: ISP DNS filters

Online gambling operates in a legal grey area in India, regulated at state level, but many Indian ISPs implement DNS-based blocks on offshore casino domains. The mirror stake.ac was specifically deployed to serve the Indian market where the primary domain triggers ISP filters. No VPN needed, no software, no workaround, just a different TLD that hasn't been targeted yet.

Phishing honeypots: how to verify a real mirror

Not every domain resembling "stake" is an official mirror. Phishing sites mimicking Stake's design exist specifically to harvest login credentials and payment details. The four markers of a legitimate mirror:

1. SSL certificate check

The padlock in your browser address bar should show a certificate issued to a domain under Medium Rare N.V.'s control. Click the padlock, open certificate details, and verify the organization name matches.

2. WHOIS registrant

All legitimate mirrors are registered by Medium Rare N.V. A WHOIS lookup on the domain (via whois.domaintools.com or similar) should reflect this. Mismatched registrants are a red flag.

3. Correct layout and game providers

Fake mirrors often have broken UI elements, missing game provider logos (Pragmatic Play, Evolution, Hacksaw), or altered deposit and withdrawal pages. The game catalog should be identical to stake.com.

4. Source of the link

Only follow mirror links from established affiliate or review sites. Never click links from unsolicited Telegram DMs, Discord messages, or social media posts claiming to share an "exclusive" mirror.
