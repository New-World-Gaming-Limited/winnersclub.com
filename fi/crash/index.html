What are crash games

Crash-pelit are a category of instant kasinopelit in which a multiplier rises from 1.00x and can end (or "crash") at any point. The player's objective is to cashout before the crash, collecting their stake multiplied by the current value. If the round ends before the player cashes out, the stake is lost.

Unlike slottis, crash games have no spinning reels or static paylines. The outcome of each round is determined by a random number generator (or provably fair seed system in the case of Spribe, SmartSoft, and Aviatrix titles) before the multiplier begins to climb. This means the crash point is fixed before you place your bet; the rising counter is a display of that predetermined value being revealed in real time.

Crash-pelit typically offer RTPs between 95% and 98%, which is higher than most video slottis. The trade-off is that variance within a session can be severe: a run of low-multiplier crashes can deplete a bankroll quickly if bet sizing is not managed. The skill element, such as it is, lies in disciplined cashout timing and stake management, not in predicting crash points.

The category has expanded from the original Aviator format to include grid-based games (Mines), ball-drop games (Plinko), and card-prediction games (HiLo), all sharing the fundamental mechanic of a rising multiplier and a cashout decision point.

What is a provably fair crash game?

A provably fair game generates round outcomes using a cryptographic seed system. The server publishes a hash of the seed before veikkaus opens. After the round, the seed is revealed and pelaajat can independently verify the hash matches and reproduce the outcome, confirming the result was not altered after bets were placed.
