Tools inside your Stake account

Settings, Responsible Gambling. Every one of these takes under sixty seconds to configure.

Deposit limit

Cap the total deposit value over a 24-hour, 7-day, or 30-day window. Decreases apply instantly; increases require a 24-hour cool-off before they take effect.

Loss limit

Cap your net loss over a chosen window. When you hit the cap, deposits and bets are blocked until the window resets.

Wager limit

Cap total amount staked (turnover), independent of wins and losses. Useful for managing time-and-volume risk rather than just net P&L.

Session limit

Cap the length of a single play session. After the limit, you're logged out and the session reset must be confirmed manually.

Reality check

An in-game pop-up that interrupts your session at chosen intervals (15, 30, 60 minutes). Shows session length, net P&L, and a "take a break" button.

Cool-off

Lock your account for a fixed period: 24 hours, 7 days, 30 days. No deposits, no bets, no withdrawals during the lock.

Self-exclusion

Long-term lock: 6 months, 1 year, 5 years, or permanent. Cannot be reversed before the period ends. The strongest in-platform tool.

Account closure

Full closure with funds returned. Stake's compliance team handles closures within the working day, faster if the request is plain and the KYC is current.

Warning signs to watch

Gambling becomes a problem when the activity is no longer pleasant, when it interferes with work, sleep, or relationships, or when bet sizes climb to "make back" losses. Common signals:

  • Chasing losses by increasing stake size or session length
  • Borrowing money or using credit to deposit
  • Lying about wins, losses, or time spent on the platform
  • Skipping work, sleep, exercise or social plans to play
  • Feeling agitated or restless when not playing
  • Net losses you cannot easily afford from disposable income

If any of these are familiar, the responsible call is to pause. Use the cool-off or self-exclusion tool inside Stake, and contact one of the helplines above. The calls are free and confidential.

A short list of house rules

Money you can lose

Treat the bankroll as the cost of entertainment. If losing all of it would meaningfully change your week, the stake is too high.

Set the limits before the session

Decide deposit, loss, and session limits in your settings before opening a game. Decisions made cold beat decisions made mid-tilt.

No drink, no chase

Alcohol erodes both bankroll discipline and bet sizing. Losses chased the same night they happen rarely come back.

Treat sleep like a hard stop

4-hour-plus sessions past your normal bedtime are statistically where most regrettable bets happen. Walk away.
