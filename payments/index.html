KYC tiers

Four levels. Not all mandatory for every player - they trigger based on what you do.

Stake runs a four-tier Know Your Customer system. Level 1 is mandatory for every account. Level 2 fires before your first withdrawal - no exceptions. Levels 3 and 4 are triggered by volume and compliance flags, not applied universally. The framework is designed so a casual depositor rarely passes Level 2; serious high-volume players will eventually hit Level 3 or 4.

LevelWhen It TriggersWhat You Submit
Level 1 Account creation - mandatory for everyone Full legal name, date of birth, residential address
Level 2 Before any withdrawal - mandatory for all withdrawers Government-issued photo ID (passport, national ID, or driver's licence, front and back) plus a selfie holding the ID - no glasses, no hat, full face and document clearly visible
Level 3 High-volume deposits or withdrawals (compliance flag) Proof of address: utility bill, bank or credit card statement, tenancy agreement, or government tax letter - all within the last six months, showing full name and residential address
Level 4 Very high-volume activity or suspicious transaction patterns Source of funds: payslips or bank statements showing regular income, investment account statements, inheritance documents, business sale records, or crypto mining ledgers (wallet address plus transaction hash). Original photographs or PDFs only - no screenshots.

Bypass is not possible at Level 2. Stake's support cannot manually override the ID check. Many players encounter Level 3 requests when a single session withdrawal exceeds approximately $10,000. Level 4 is typically reserved for accounts moving six-figure-plus volumes. The 24-hour turnaround for Level 2 document reviews is standard; complex Level 4 reviews can extend beyond this.
